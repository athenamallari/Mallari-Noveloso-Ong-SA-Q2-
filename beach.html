Paliton Beach

Paliton Beach is a Siquijor’s Premium destination for stunning white sand, crystal clear water, and a beautiful tropical scenery perfect for pictures. Tucked away on the island's west coast in San Juan. It earned its nickname “Little Boracay” for its breathtaking beauty and serene atmosphere. Towering coconut palms line the shore, casting natural shade and framing idyllic views of the ocean, contributing to the beach's relaxed atmosphere.

Salagdoong Beach

Salagdoong Beach, located on Siquijor's eastern coastline in the town of Maria, is where visitors go for an exciting adventure rather than a peaceful relaxation spot. One of the main attractions is a famous set of cliff-diving platforms, offering thrill-seekers the chance to leap from 6 to 7 meters into a stunning vibrant turquoise water below. Two small, charming coves form the beach area, which is run as a public resort. Visitors can find basic facilities like a restaurant and tables for hire. This vibrant location provides great opportunities for snorkeling and swimming, mixing a family atmosphere with an optional adrenaline rush.

Tubod Beach

Tubod Beach, located in San Juan, Siquijor, is mainly famous for the Tubod Marine Sanctuary, a conservation zone created only a few meters from the coastline. This refuge is a snorkeler's dream, featuring a flourishing and intact ecosystem of bright coral reefs, seagrass meadows, and an impressive variety of marine creatures, including vivid fish, large clams, blue starfish, and regular sea turtle appearances. The water remains consistently clear, tranquil, and shallow, making it an ideal location for both beginner snorkelers and seasoned snorkeler. Tourists can readily reach the specific snorkeling routes identified by buoys, hire excellent equipment from the adjacent dive shop or local sellers, and discover this vibrant underwater realm, which showcases Siquijor's effective conservation initiatives.
